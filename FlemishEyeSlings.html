Flemish Eye Construction Process

Step-by-step manufacturing of our premium slings

1

Blinding and Splitting

Blind the rope from the end at distance equal to desired loop length plus 10 times rope diameter. Split by separating three adjacent strands.

2

Forming the Loop

Bend the larger section (three strands + core) into desired loop size. Lay the separated strands into grooves as close to the throat as possible.

3

Strand Placement

Completely lay the three strands around the loop deep into the throat, allowing six-strand ends and core to project near the throat.

4

Ferrule Application

Position special ferrule over the rolled-in splice in preparation for swaging. Ends remain flat during this process.

5

Swaging Process

Apply tremendous force with swaging machine to taper the ferrule, creating a permanent, high-strength connection.

Tucking & Finishing Process

Precision techniques for maximum durability

Method of Tucking

The tucks of the splice are made over one strand and under one strand against the lay of the rope. The heart strand of the rope is not removed when strands are opened for tucking, but may be cut to a suitable length.

Professional Finishing

The ends of the splice are tightly served with seizing wire after parceling with canvas, hessian or wax paper. This ensures maximum durability and protection against environmental factors.

This professional finishing process creates a smooth, streamlined connection that minimizes wear and maximizes the lifespan of your slings.
